Animal Farm (Main Focus)

  1. Explain how the pigs' behaviour in this extract differs from the original ideals of Animalism.
  2. How does Orwell show that power has changed the pigs? Refer to two details.
  3. Why do the other animals struggle to challenge the pigs' authority? Give a reason.
1. The pigs now act like humans and place themselves above the others. 2. They carry whips, live in the farmhouse, and take privileges. 3. The animals have been conditioned to obey and fear questioning power.

Original Skin

  1. Describe the emotional shift that occurs in the poem.
  2. How does the poet create a sense of unity in the crowd?
  3. What warning does the poet express at the end?
1. The poem moves from unity and hope to violence and regret. 2. Through shared singing, raised fists, and a common purpose. 3. That uncontrolled anger leads to repeated cycles of destruction.

The Butterfly

  1. What does the butterfly symbolize in this context?
  2. How does the poet contrast beauty with suffering?
  3. Why is the final line powerful?
1. It symbolizes freedom and life beyond the ghetto. 2. Bright natural imagery contrasts with harsh living conditions. 3. It shows that hope has disappeared completely.

There Is No Time

  1. What urgent message is expressed in the poem?
  2. Identify a phrase repeated and explain its purpose.
  3. How does the poet create a fast pace?
1. Action must happen immediately; life demands effort. 2. "There is no time" emphasizes urgency. 3. Short lines and repetition increase pressure.

Nothing But The Truth

  1. How do the characters differ in their emotional expression?
  2. What conflict is shown between personal grief and cultural expectation?
  3. What theme about truth is revealed?
1. One expresses emotions openly; the other represses them. 2. The pressure to mourn a certain way creates tension. 3. The truth is difficult but necessary for healing.
